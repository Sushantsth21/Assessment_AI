
Pneumonia - CDC Information

KEY POINTS
- Pneumonia is a lung infection caused by bacteria, viruses, fungi, or parasites.
- Types include community-acquired, healthcare-acquired, and ventilator-associated pneumonia.
- Common symptoms: chest pain, cough, fever, fatigue, nausea, shortness of breath, altered mental status.
- Risk increases with age, chronic illnesses (diabetes, lung disease, etc.), smoking, and exposure to infected individuals.
- Bacterial causes: Mycoplasma pneumoniae, pneumococcal disease.
- Viral causes: COVID-19, influenza, RSV, HPIV, HMPV, rhinovirus.
- Fungal and parasitic causes are less common.
- Prevention includes vaccines (Hib, pneumococcal, flu, RSV, COVID-19), good hygiene, and managing chronic conditions.
- World Pneumonia Day: November 12.

See CDC for more at: https://www.cdc.gov/pneumonia
